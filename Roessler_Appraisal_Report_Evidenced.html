(1) Overview & (2) Objectives

Item Appraisal Answer Verbatim Evidence from Paper
Objective Examine effect of exercise as adjunct to AUD treatment at 6/12 months. Page 3 (Objective) "The objective of the present study is to examine whether physical activity as an adjunct to outpatient alcohol treatment has an effect on alcohol intake at six months and 12 months after treatment initiation..."
Inclusion Criteria ICD-10 AUD; 18+; Danish speaking; no psychosis/cognitive impairment. Page 5 (Participants) "...fulfilling ICD-10 criteria for harmful use of or dependence on alcohol, age over 18 years, Danish speaking, no severe psychosis or cognitive impairment, no severe physical disabilities..."

(3) Participants & Groups

Item Appraisal Answer Verbatim Evidence from Paper
Groups Group Exercise, Individual Exercise, Control (TAU). Page 3 (Design) "...(A) Patients allocated to treatment as usual, (B) Patients allocated to treatment as usual and supervised group exercise, (C) Patients allocated to treatment as usual and individual physical exercise."
Baseline n Total recruited: 175. Page 5 (Participants) "A total of 175 consecutive participants were recruited from the two outpatient alcohol treatment centers in Odense (n = 148) and Svendborg (n = 27)..."

(4) Outcomes & Stats

Item Appraisal Answer Verbatim Evidence from Paper
Primary Outcome Proportion of patients not drinking excessively at 6 months. Page 5 (Outcome measures) "The primary outcome measure was the proportion of patients who did not drink excessively six months after treatment start."
Sample Size Calc Planned n=300 (Underpowered). Protocol Page 4 (Sample size) "A sample of 100 patients in each group is needed to have 90% power... Due to the power calculation and the high dropout experiences from earlier studies, it is necessary to include all 300 patients in the study."
Analysis Type Intention-to-Treat (ITT). Page 5 (Analysis) "Analysis of outcome was based on intention-to-treat, irrespective of whether the patients had dropped out of the intervention."

(5) Results

Item Appraisal Answer Verbatim Evidence from Paper
Primary Results No significant effect found (OR ~1). Page 8 (Primary outcome) "The OR 0.99 for excessive drinking in the group exercise condition, and 1.02 in the individual exercise condition, which, when compared to the control group as reference, did not differ statistically significantly."
Dose-Response 4% decrease per exercising day. Page 9 (Dose-response) "Amount of alcohol consumption in the intervention groups decreased by 4% [95% CI: 0.03–6.8%], p = 0.015 for each increased exercising day."
Moderate Activity OR 0.12 (Protective). Page 9 (Primary outcome) "Participants with moderate level physical activity had lower odds for excessive drinking, OR = 0.12 [0.05; 0.31], p<0.001..."
