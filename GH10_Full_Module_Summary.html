1. The Research Funnel

Evidence-Informed Decision Making (EiDM) is the process of using the best available research to make healthcare choices. We select literature using a two-step approach:

Step 1: Ti/Ab Screening

Quickly reading Titles and Abstracts to discard irrelevant studies.

Step 2: Full-Text Screening

Reading the entire paper to ensure it meets your specific criteria.

3. Prioritizing Endpoints (GRADE)

Outcomes (Endpoints) are ranked 1 to 9 based on their importance for decision-making from the patient's perspective.

RankCategoryExamples
7-9EssentialMortality, major illness, quality of life.
4-6ImportantSide effects, minor symptoms, hospital stay duration.
1-3Not ImportantLaboratory markers (HbA1c, Cortisol), surrogate outcomes.
4. Case Study: The "Running Problem"

Study: Roessler et al. 2017 (Exercise for Alcohol Use Disorder)

  • Design: Randomized Controlled Trial (RCT).
  • Finding: Simply *assigning* people to exercise had no effect (Intention-to-Treat).
  • Finding: People who *actually* exercised showed a massive reduction in drinking (Dose-Response).
  • Lesson: Evidence must distinguish between the "Offer" of treatment and the "Action" of treatment.
5. Critical Appraisal Tools

Used to ensure Randomized Controlled Trials (RCTs) are reported transparently. It checks if the "Fair Test" was actually fair.
