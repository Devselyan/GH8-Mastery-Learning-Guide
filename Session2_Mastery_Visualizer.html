Deep-Dive into Confidence Intervals & Estimation

Phase 4: Anatomy of a Confidence Interval

A Confidence Interval (CI) is our safety net. It recognizes that the "Sample Mean" is just an estimate, and the real "Population Mean" is likely somewhere nearby.

CI = Point Estimate ± (Margin of Error)

The 95% Level means that if we repeat the study 100 times, we expect 95 of our intervals to capture the "True" value. It is a measure of reliability, not probability.

Phase 5: Precision vs. Sample Size

The Narrow Interval

High Precision

Usually occurs with Large Sample Sizes ($N$). We are very confident in where the true value lies.

The Wide Interval

Low Precision

Usually occurs with Small Sample Sizes ($N$). There is a lot of "noise" or uncertainty.
