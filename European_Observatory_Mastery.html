European Observatory on Health Systems and Policies

01: What is the Observatory?

Founded in 1998, the European Observatory on Health Systems and Policies is a high-level partnership that acts as a "knowledge broker." It bridges the gap between scientific evidence and health policy-making across the European region.


It is hosted by the WHO Regional Office for Europe and operates with the core mission of promoting evidence-informed policy-making through rigorous analysis of health systems, trends, and reforms.

03: Core Products & Outputs
HiT (Health Systems in Transition) Profiles The "gold standard" of health system analysis. Standardized, deep-dive reports on individual national health systems (e.g., Germany: Health System Review).
HSPM (Health Systems and Policy Monitor) An online platform for real-time tracking of health reforms and comparative cross-country data analysis.
Policy Briefs & Dialogues Concise evidence summaries for urgent policy questions, complemented by workshops where experts meet directly with national decision-makers.
Eurohealth A quarterly academic and policy journal providing a platform for discussing emerging health issues.
04: Current Analytical Priorities (2024-2028)
  • Health Workforce: Managing shortages, migration, and retention of medical professionals.
  • Digital Health & AI: Governance of Artificial Intelligence and implementation of electronic health records.
  • Resilience & Crisis: Learning from COVID-19 to build systems capable of withstanding future shocks.
  • Integrated Care: Improving coordination between primary care, social services, and long-term care.
  • Sustainability: Addressing the "Green" health system and reducing the carbon footprint of delivery.
  • Access to Innovation: Strategies for sustainable pricing of high-cost innovative medicines.
