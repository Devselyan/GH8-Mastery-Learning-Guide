Critical Appraisal: Roessler et al. 2017

Analysis prepared for discussion on 15th June 2026.

(1) Overview of the Publication and Study

TitleExercise as adjunctive treatment for alcohol use disorder: A randomized controlled trial
AuthorsRoessler, K. K., Bilberg, R., Søgaard Nielsen, A., Jensen, K., Ekstrøm, C. T., & Sari, S.
JournalPLOS ONE (p. 1)
Year2017
Study TypeThree-arm Randomized Controlled Trial (parallel)
CountryDenmark
SettingClinical (outpatient alcohol treatment centers in Odense and Svendborg) (p. 4, Setting)
Study Duration6 months intervention; 12 months follow-up (p. 3, Design)

(2) Objectives and Selection Criteria (P)

ObjectiveTo examine whether physical activity as an adjunct to outpatient alcohol treatment has an effect on alcohol intake at 6 and 12 months (p. 3, Objective)
Inclusion CriteriaICD-10 criteria for harmful use/dependence; age 18-65; Danish speaking; no severe psychosis/cognitive impairment (p. 5, Participants)
Exclusion CriteriaSevere physical disabilities or medical problems (p. 5, Participants)

(3) Participants (P), Intervention (I) and Comparison (C)

Groups Group A: Control (TAU) Group B: Supervised Group Exercise Group C: Individual Exercise
Participants (Baseline) n = 53 n = 62 n = 60
Analysed (ITT) n = 172 (3 excluded due to missing baseline interview) (p. 5)
Lost-to-follow up 37.1% at 6 months (p. 11, Strengths and limitations)
Baseline Age (Mean) 46.9 (SD 11.6) 44.8 (SD 11.2) 43.8 (SD 11.1)
Baseline Sex (Male %) 73.6% 59.0% 77.6%
Intervention Description Standard treatment alone; asked not to change activity levels. TAU + Supervised group exercise (2x/week, 60 mins) for 24 weeks. TAU + Individual running program; advised 2x/week.

(4) Outcome Parameters (O) and Statistical Procedures

Primary OutcomeProportion of patients not drinking excessively (abstinent or moderate) at 6 months (p. 5, Outcome measures)
InstrumentsTimeline Follow-back (TLFB) and Addiction Severity Index (ASI) (p. 4, Measures)
Statistical ProceduresLogistic regression for primary outcome; ITT with LOCF (p. 5, Analysis)
Planned Power (Beta)90% (from Protocol, p. 4)
Sample Size CalculationYes; Planned n=300 (100 per group) (from Protocol, p. 4)
Actual Sample Sizen = 175 (p. 1) - Study was underpowered.

(5) Results

Point Estimates (6 mo) OR 0.99 [95% CI: 0.46; 2.14] for Group Exercise
OR 1.02 [95% CI: 0.47; 2.18] for Individual Exercise
(p. 8, Table 3)
Key FindingNo statistically significant effect of exercise intervention on primary drinking outcome compared to TAU.
Dose-ResponseAlcohol consumption decreased by 4% for each increased exercising day (p. 9, Dose-response).

(8) Methodological Criteria

RandomizationYes. Independently performed urn-randomization (p. 3).
Allocation ConcealmentUnclear/Adequate. Stated as "independently performed" and "research team... do not know outcome in advance" (Protocol p. 2).
BlindingNo. Not possible for exercise intervention; participants and staff were aware of group assignment.
ITT AnalysisYes. Used Last Observation Carried Forward (LOCF) for missing data (p. 5).

(9) Overall Assessment of Study Quality

  • Study Power: Major limitation. Planned for 300, only recruited 175. This increases the risk of a Type II error (failing to detect a true effect).
  • Adherence/Dropout: High dropout rate (37.1%) and lack of reported adherence data (p. 11) makes interpretation difficult.
  • External Validity: High. Conducted in real-world outpatient settings with consecutive recruitment (p. 11).
  • Conclusion for Discussion: While the RCT failed to show a direct effect of the intervention groups, the dose-response effect and the protective nature of moderate physical activity (OR 0.12) suggest that lifestyle changes are beneficial if sustained.
